# Application Checklist

A pre-flight checklist for credit program applications. Run through this once and you'll pass 80% of automated approval checks across every major program.

## Before You Apply to Anything

### Company Presence

- [ ] **Live company website** on your own domain. Not a Carrd page, not a Notion share link. A real site describing what you build, who it's for, and who's on the team.
- [ ] **Custom domain email** matching your website. `founder@yourcompany.com`, not `yourcompany.founder@gmail.com`. Google Workspace or Microsoft 365 — either works.
- [ ] **Company LinkedIn page** with at least the company logo, description, and founder(s) listed. MongoDB and several others explicitly check this.
- [ ] **GitHub organization** (not personal account) if you're applying to developer-focused programs.

### Incorporation and Finance

- [ ] **Incorporated entity.** Delaware C-corp for US-targeting startups; local equivalents elsewhere. NVIDIA Inception and most others require an incorporation date.
- [ ] **Business bank account** separate from personal finances. Mercury and Brex are common choices; traditional banks work fine too.
- [ ] **EIN or equivalent** tax ID.

### Application Content

- [ ] **Pitch deck** (even a 5-slide version). Inception, Anthropic, and Google Scale tier ask for this.
- [ ] **One-paragraph product description** that clearly explains what you build. Reviewers scan these — jargon-heavy descriptions get flagged.
- [ ] **Accurate funding stage.** "Pre-seed," "seed," "Series A," or "bootstrapped" — use the term that matches your most recent round. Don't inflate.
- [ ] **Most recent funding date** and lead investor name if applicable.
- [ ] **Investor or accelerator Organization ID** if you have affiliation. This single field is the difference between $1K and $100K+ on AWS and Google.

## Per-Provider Specifics

### AWS Activate

- [ ] New AWS account in the company name (not linked to a previous rejected Activate application).
- [ ] Account on Paid Tier Plan (not the default free-only plan).
- [ ] Business email tied to company domain.
- [ ] Correct package selected: Founders if bootstrapped, Portfolio if you have an Activate Provider Org ID.

### Google for Startups Cloud

- [ ] 18-character Google Cloud billing account ID ready.
- [ ] Company domain matches email domain matches billing account email.
- [ ] Clear answer on whether AI is foundational to your product (not a bolted-on feature) if applying to the AI tier.

### Microsoft Founders Hub

- [ ] New Azure customer (no prior subscriptions on the account).
- [ ] Not a consultancy, agency, or crypto-mining business.
- [ ] Clear software-based product.
- [ ] If applying via a VC referral, have the partner code ready.

### NVIDIA Inception

- [ ] Incorporated under 10 years ago.
- [ ] At least one developer on the team.
- [ ] Not a consulting firm, crypto company, cloud reseller, or public company.
- [ ] Pitch deck ready to attach.

### Anthropic Startup Program

- [ ] Clear AI use case with real or projected API usage.
- [ ] If pursuing the Anthology Fund path, be prepared for 1–2 video interviews after the written application.
- [ ] Willing to sign compliance affidavit and publicity release within 14 days of acceptance.

### MongoDB for Startups

- [ ] Series A or earlier funding stage.
- [ ] Live website and active company LinkedIn.
- [ ] Not an agency, consultancy, or design firm.

## Red Flags That Trigger Rejection

These will get you rejected or delayed on almost every program. Fix them before applying:

- **Gmail/Yahoo/Outlook personal email** as primary contact. Immediate credibility hit.
- **Website that redirects to a landing page builder's default domain** (e.g., `yourcompany.carrd.co`).
- **"Coming soon" page** with no actual product description.
- **No LinkedIn presence** at all.
- **Mismatched information** — website says one thing, application says another, LinkedIn says a third.
- **Consulting/agency language** on your site if you're applying to programs that exclude services firms.
- **Reusing an AWS account** that was previously rejected for Activate.
- **Applying to multiple tiers of the same program simultaneously** hoping one sticks.

## After Submission

- [ ] **Track expected response times.** AWS Activate: 7–10 business days. Google: a few business days to 10+. Microsoft: immediate to a few days. Anthropic Anthology Fund: ~2 weeks to first response.
- [ ] **Check spam/promotions folders** daily for the first two weeks after submitting. Approval emails frequently get filtered.
- [ ] **If rejected, wait 3+ months** before reapplying, and only after materially improving the application (traction, funding, product maturity). Same-profile resubmissions rarely flip.
- [ ] **Once approved, claim the credit promptly** — some programs require activation within 30–90 days or credits are voided.

## Credit Hygiene Once You Have Them

- [ ] **Set billing alerts and budgets** on day one. Credits hide spend; when they expire, you don't want a surprise bill.
- [ ] **Tag resources** so you can identify which workloads are eating credits fastest.
- [ ] **Track credit expiration dates** in a shared doc. Most credits expire 12 months from grant.
- [ ] **Don't over-provision** just because credits are "free." Cloud providers know exactly how much you'd pay post-credits, and the pricing shock is real.
- [ ] **Model your unit economics assuming zero credits.** If the business only works with credits, the business doesn't work.
